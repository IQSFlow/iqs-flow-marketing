— The five dimensions

What actually gets measured.

Weights were chosen to reflect what operations leaders told us actually matters at renewal. Every dimension normalizes by site count so small vendors aren't penalized for scale.

30%

Quality

Average inspection score across the rubric, weighted by site and normalized by framework tier.

25%

Evidence

Percentage of inspections with complete, photo-verified evidence per required checklist item.

20%

Compliance

Framework-scored performance: ATA 103, JCAHO, OSHA, ISSA, CDC EVS, ISO 14644 as applicable.

15%

Response

Time from finding to remediation. Weighted inversely by severity tier of the flagged item.

10%

Consistency

Standard deviation of score across sites and shifts, penalizes volatility, not absolute performance.

VAS = 0.30·Q + 0.25·E + 0.20·C + 0.15·R + 0.10·σ−1
Q quality · E evidence · C compliance · R response · σ−1 inverse volatility · normalized 0-100

— Methodology questions

How we keep it honest.

Who reviews the methodology?

The weights and normalization are reviewed annually by an external advisory board of operations directors (airlines, hospitals, REITs). Any change is published with version number, effective date, and a back-computed comparison against the previous method.

Can a vendor dispute a finding?

Yes. Any flagged inspection has a 72-hour appeal window in which the vendor can submit counter-evidence. Appeals are adjudicated by the customer's configured reviewer, not by IQS. Appeal outcomes are part of the public audit trail.

How does site size affect the score?

Every dimension is computed per-site and then averaged, so a vendor with 3 well-run sites isn't punished for having less data than a vendor with 30. Consistency (σ−1) uses a minimum-observation threshold to avoid noise at low counts.

Is this published as an open standard?

The methodology is published here and the computation code for dimensions Q, E, and R is open-source under MIT. Compliance mapping (dimension C) is proprietary to our framework libraries and updated on their cadence.

Can we use our own weights?

Enterprise customers can override weights for internal use with our in-product weight editor. The published VAS always uses the canonical weights so cross-portfolio comparisons remain comparable.
